Introduction to Search Indexing

Published: July 10, 2025 | Author: Jane Smith

Search indexing is a fundamental concept in information retrieval systems. It involves organizing data in a way that facilitates fast and accurate search operations.

Why Indexing Matters

Without proper indexing, searching through large volumes of text would be prohibitively slow. Indexes allow search engines to quickly locate documents containing specific terms.

Common Indexing Techniques

Several techniques are commonly used in search indexing, including:

  • Inverted indexes
  • N-gram indexes
  • Vector space models
